About

For decades, Coral Reefs have been negatively affected by Climate change due to a drastic change in ocean temperatures and aggressive pollution rates. While this may not affect everyone in the world just yet, millions of people are still heavily dependent on Coral Reefs and their health for natural resources, their daily lives, and event employment. The irreversible problems are already extremely prevalent. Society needs to come to terms with the fact that this issue cannot be ignored any longer, and change is needed immediately. In addition, Coral reefs are vital for our oceans, supporting many different types of marine species, but also providing livelihoods for over 600 million people, and coastal protection for millions more. Furthermore, people who live on small islands are heavily impacted by the changing ocean temperatures, despite the fact that this seemingly irrelevant issue can destroy everything from their jobs at small businesses, to physical health and overall well being. Smaller communities being affected is just the beginning of this global issue, and soon major cities will begin to see the change in their lives from the lack of healthy, thriving coral reefs. Major coastal areas that rely on tourism such as Hawaii, California, and Australia need healthy vibrant oceans in order to keep the business alive. Without the reefs, tourism dies down along with the economy in these areas. The Great Barrier Reef, which for many years has been known as a beautiful tourist site, has lost thousands of visitors since the coral began to quickly and visibly bleach several years ago. This is only the beginning of the economic effects coral bleaching has on the world. While some parts of the globe may be working toward change, reefs can still be easily destroyed if there is pollution somewhere else in the ocean thousands of miles away from the reef itself. Mankind needs to realize it is time to take charge and move towards fixing this issue if we want our planet to prosper instead of being slowly destroyed through our own careless actions. Coral Reefs dying is just the beginning, and sooner than later Climate Change will be beyond the point of repair, and human life will be in as much danger as coral reefs.

The Bright Colors Distract

Coral reefs are brightly colored and beautiful to look at and because they are so vibrant and they look lively many people think they are healthy and nothing is wrong. People treat these coral reefs as a fun activity to do on vacation or just a pretty sight to see. What people do not know is that when these coral reefs are so vibrantly colored it is a sign of death and commonly means that the reefs are in early signs of a bleaching event. These coral reefs are beautiful and everyone thinks that because of this it means they are healthy, but they are actually in the stages of dying. Many of the photos that are on the internet are also heavily photoshopped to brighten up the colors and make them look more appealing to the world. The photos have artificial lighting, pumped up color, and are even heavily edited in documentaries. These edited pictures are what makes the whole world think these coral reefs are healthy and that there is nothing they should be doing to help.

Rising Ocean Temperatures are “Cooking” Coral Reefs

In recent years, we have seen the worst and most damaging coral bleaching of all time. Rising water temperatures have been so extreme that it caused some coral reefs to “cook”, turning them to dust and stopping their positive benefits. This could severely harm our environment if nothing is done to further prevent it and it continues to spread. Countries like France are transitioning away from fossil fuels and into more sustainable energy to fight climate change, and hopefully other countries will follow to maintain positive global coral reef health and everyone can continue to profit from their benefits.

How they got so bad

The harm that is being caused to these coral reefs can be traced back to humans in more ways than one. The main causes of these coral reefs dying include climate change, declining water quality, overfishing, pollution, and unsustainable coastal development. Some causes that are directly related to humans are overfishing and destructive fishing practices using dynamite or cyanide. Cyanide is a chemical that kills coral polyps and algae and it turns many coral reefs, which are also known as the “rainforests of the ocean”, into marine deserts. “A square meter of reef is destroyed for every live fish caught using cyanide.” says biologist Sam Mamauag of the international marinelife alliance. Another very harmful thing that humans do is collecting live healthy corals for the aquarium market and leaving the unhealthy and dying ones in the ocean. Humans have a large part in harming these coral reefs and many need to be educated on how they are harming them in order to try and stop and help try and keep these coral reefs healthy.
