About

For decades, Coral Reefs have been negatively affected by Climate change due to drastic change in ocean temperatures. While this may not affect everyone in the world just yet, millions of people are still heavily dependent on Coral Reefs for natural resources and their daily lives, and have already seen the irreversible problems. Coral reefs are vital for our oceans, supporting many different types of marine species, but also providing livelihoods for over 600 million people, and coastal protection for millions more. People who live on small islands are impacted by the changing ocean temperatures, despite the fact that this seemingly irrelevant issue can destroy everything from their jobs at small businesses, to physical health and overall well being. Smaller communities being affected is just the beginning of this global issue, and soon major cities will begin to see the change in their lives from the lack of healthy, thriving coral reefs. Major coastal areas that rely on tourism such as Hawaii, California, and Australia need healthy vibrant oceans in order to keep the business thriving. Without the reefs, tourism dies down along with the economy in these areas. The Great Barrier Reef, which for years has been a beautiful tourist site, has lost thousands of visitors since the coral began to quickly and visibly bleach several years ago. While some parts of the world may be working toward change, reefs can still be destroyed even if there is pollution in the ocean thousands of miles away from the reef. Society needs to realize it is time to take charge and move towards fixing this issue if we want our planet to prosper instead of being slowly destroyed through our own careless actions. Coral Reefs dying is just the beginning, and sooner than later Climate Change will be beyond the point of repair, and human life will be in as much danger as coral reefs.

The Bright Colors Distract

Coral reefs are brightly colored and beautiful to look at and because they are so vibrant and they look lively many people think they are healthy and nothing is wrong. People treat these coral reefs as a fun activity to do on vacation or just a pretty sight to see. What people do not know is that when these coral reefs are so vibrantly colored it is a sign of death and commonly means that the reefs are in early signs of a bleaching event. These coral reefs are beautiful and everyone thinks that because of this it means they are healthy but they are actually in the stages of dying. Many of the photos that are on the internet are also heavily photoshopped to brighten up the colors and make them look more appealing to the world. The photos have artificial lighting, pumped up color, and even heavily edited in documentaries. These edited pictures are what is mankind the whole world think these coral reefs are healthy and that there is nothing they should be doing to help.

Climate change, global warming and coral reefs: Modelling the effects of temperature

Click here to open this PDF in a new window


This document shows how the growth rate of species of coral has decreased because of an increase in sea surface temperatures, and this increase of temperatures also result in thermal bleaching on the coral which causes additional energy demands on the coral, depleted reserves, reduced biomass, and also death. This can cause major issues because about a third of marine fish species live in coral reefs and with the effects of an increase in temperature hurting or killing them off that causes a huge disruption in the environment and the food chain.
